    ~~~~~~~~~~  SUNSET EXPRESS  ~~~~~~~~~~
// ABOARD THE SUNSET EXPRESS — COYOTE CREEK TERRITORY //

The train screams to a halt. Outside, the desert sky is streaked with crimson. Inside, the passenger car erupts in chaos as masked bandits swarm the aisles — bandoliers crossed over dusty serapes, revolvers glinting. Their leader, a man in a skull mask with a pearl-handled Colt, bellows over the panic: "¡Manos arriba! This is a robbery! Cooperate and nobody dies."

A nun in the seat ahead of you clutches a small leather case. A young cowboy across the aisle reaches for his holster. The bandit nearest you has turned his back. In the distant mail car, you hear the thump of dynamite charges being set.

// The Sunset Express carries not just passengers, but a secret government gold shipment. Everyone on this train has a story. And the next minute will decide yours. //


            // PASSENGER CAR — GUNSMOKE AND CHAOS //
            

                
Your Colt clears leather before the bandit can turn. The shot takes him in the shoulder, spinning him into a seat. The car erupts — bullets splinter wood, passengers scream, the nun dives for cover. You exchange fire with two more bandits, dropping both, but the leader in the skull mask — El Muerto — grabs the nun and holds a blade to her throat. "Drop it, gringo, or she dies."

                
Behind you, the young cowboy fires from his seat, wounding another bandit. In the confusion, the nun kicks El Muerto in the knee and breaks free, pulling a derringer from her sleeve. She fires point-blank, but El Muerto escapes through the mail car, blowing the door with a dynamite charge and leaping from the train. The remaining bandits flee after him, leaving a trail of blood and gold dust.

                
The nun turns to you, breathless. "Agent Isabella Reyes, Pinkerton Detective Agency. That man has information I need. Thank you — you just saved my life." She eyes the wounded cowboy. "We'll reach Coyote Creek in an hour. There's a saloon, a sheriff, and a railroad man named McClane who wants to string us all up. Who do you want to trust?"

                
// You're a hero now. But McClane, the railroad's security chief, is already suspicious of anyone who fought the bandits and lived. //

            

            

                
                
                
            

        


        
        

            // PASSENGER CAR — UNDER THE BANDITS' THUMB //
            

                
You raise your hands and let them take your coin purse. The skull-masked leader — El Muerto — pauses before you, his dark eyes studying your face. "You got sense, amigo. Not many do." He tosses you a heavy gold coin. "We need men who can follow orders. The next big score is the Golden Spike Express — an entire railcar of gold. You want in, meet us at the old Spanish mission by Coyote Creek. Ask for Culebra. Tell him El Muerto sent you."

                
When the gang leaves, the passengers murmur. A nun — Agent Isabella Reyes, though you don't know it yet — watches you closely. The young cowboy curses you for a coward. But you have a gold coin and an invitation to the biggest train robbery the West has ever seen.

                
// El Muerto saw something in you. Whether he saw a partner or a pawn is up to you. //

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // TRAIN ROOF — WIND AND SPEED //
            

                
You wriggle through the window and haul yourself onto the roof. The train is still moving, the wind howling. From up here, you see the bandits riding away down a dry wash. The mail car is a wreck of splinters and smoke. You spot a lone figure crawling along the roof toward you — the young cowboy from inside, blood streaming from a graze on his forehead. "They killed the marshal's son," he gasps. "I'm riding for help."

                
He leaps from the train as it slows on a curve, vanishing into the brush. You jump too, landing hard in a sandy arroyo. When you stagger into Coyote Creek hours later, you're met by an old man with a sawed-off shotgun on the porch of a dilapidated ranch. Buckshot Johnny, they call him. "You look like you've been chewed up and spit out, son. Come inside. We got bandits to kill and a score to settle."

                
// Johnny's son was killed by El Muerto six months ago. He's been waiting for someone with guts to help him exact revenge. //

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // COYOTE CREEK — SECRET MEETING BEHIND THE CHURCH //
            

                
Agent Reyes reveals the gold shipment is a trap set by railroad baron Silas McClane. He hired El Muerto's gang to rob the train, planning to let them steal fake gold and then slaughter them to claim insurance money. But El Muerto got wind of the double-cross and intends to take the real gold instead. "If we can intercept the train first," Isabella says, "we can capture both McClane and El Muerto. I need a partner who can handle a gun."

                
She hands you a map showing the route through Dead Horse Canyon. The Golden Spike Express will pass through at dawn. You can either stake out the canyon and wait for the train, or ambush the gang before they reach the tracks.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // LAST CHANCE SALOON — WHISKEY AND FISTS //
            

                
The saloon stinks of cheap tobacco and tension. At the bar, three of El Muerto's scouts — Culebra, Oso, and a one-eyed gunman called Pico — are drinking heavily. They recognize you from the train. "The gringo who almost got us killed," Culebra snarls. "I think we finish what we started."

                
The next few seconds are a blur of broken glass and swinging fists. You duck a bottle, flip a table, and grab a pool cue. The sheriff bursts in, but you're too busy trading blows. When the dust settles, Culebra is unconscious, Oso is nursing a broken nose, and Pico is aiming a derringer at your heart. "You got sand," he admits, lowering the gun. "Maybe you're not so bad. The boss wants to meet the one who stood up to him. Come to the mission at midnight."

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // MARSHAL'S OFFICE — DEPUTY STAR //
            

                
Marshal Garrett pins a star on your vest. "Welcome to the law, son. McClane's already howling for blood — he wants to hang every bandit in the territory. But I reckon there's more to this robbery than meets the eye. You ride with my posse to Dead Horse Canyon. We'll catch the gang before they hit the gold train."

                
As you leave, McClane stops you. "I know you fought them on the train," he says coldly. "But if you cross me, you'll hang alongside them." His private guards — hired guns, not lawmen — stare at you with dead eyes.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // OLD SPANISH MISSION — BANDIT HIDEOUT //
            

                
El Muerto welcomes you with a ritual: you must drink tequila mixed with gunpowder. "From now on, you are mi hermano de sangre." He lays out the plan for the Golden Spike Express: dynamite on the trestle bridge, horses waiting in the canyon, and a getaway route through Apache territory. The gold will be split evenly — but McClane, he says, will be framed for the robbery. "That gringo thinks he's using us. But we are using him."

                
You're given a sawed-off shotgun and a horse. The heist is set for dawn. But one of the gang, a man called Tomas, pulls you aside. "Don't trust the boss. He killed the last gringo who joined us. Keep your eyes open."

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // COYOTE CREEK — DEAL WITH A PINKERTON //
            

                
You find Agent Reyes at the church, where she's been waiting. "You're the one who cooperated on the train," she says sharply. "Convince me you're not a bandit." You show her the gold coin and explain your plan to infiltrate the gang. She smiles. "I knew I could use someone like you. Fine. Get into the mission, learn their plans, and signal me with a lantern from the bell tower. Do that, and I'll make sure you get a full pardon and a reward."

                
With your new mission, you ride to the mission. But Tomas, the suspicious gang member, is already watching.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // JOHNNY'S RANCH — SAWED-OFF TRAINING //
            

                
Buckshot Johnny is a legend in his own time — retired now, but his hands haven't forgotten the weight of a shotgun. For a week, he drills you in quick-draw, tracking, and survival. "El Muerto killed my boy," he says, his voice gravel. "I'm too old for a straight fight, but I got a plan. My granddaughter, Maria, works in the saloon. She's been feeding me information. The gang will hit the gold train at Dead Horse Canyon. We'll be waiting with dynamite and the Lord on our side."

                
He hands you a custom-loaded shotgun — "Judge and Jury" — with two barrels: one loaded with buckshot, the other with a flare to blind horsemen. "Make every shot count."

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // MISSION SANTA VIOLETA — SISTER ISABELLA //
            

                
The nun from the train — Sister Isabella — is not what she seems. She meets you in the confessional, her voice low. "I'm a Pinkerton agent. The bandits stole something from the mail car — a coded letter that proves McClane's involvement. They're hiding in the old mission outside town. If you can retrieve that letter, we can bring down the whole conspiracy."

                
She slides a small derringer through the grille. "Join the gang or sneak in. But get me that letter before dawn."

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // DEAD HORSE CANYON — DUST BEFORE DAWN //
            

                
You and Isabella crouch behind boulders as the Golden Spike Express chugs into the canyon. Below, El Muerto's gang has already dynamited the trestle bridge — the train screeches to a halt. Gunfire erupts. "Now!" Isabella shouts, and you slide down the slope, picking off gunmen one by one. In the chaos, McClane's men appear from the opposite ridge — they were waiting to kill the gang and claim the gold. A three-way firefight ensues.

                
El Muerto, realizing he's trapped, pulls a stick of dynamite and charges the mail car. "If I can't have the gold, nobody can!" he screams.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // JASMINE WASH — AMBUSH AT DAWN //
            

                
You catch El Muerto's gang moving through a narrow wash. With Isabella's rifle and your revolver, you pick them off from the ridge. Culebra falls. Oso falls. Pico throws down his gun and begs for mercy. El Muerto, however, vanishes into a cave. "He's gone to the old mine," Pico gasps. "There's a back entrance. He's got dynamite stored there."

                
Isabella wants to follow. "The coded letter is still with him. We need it."

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // OLD MISSION — POSSE ASSAULT //
            

                
Marshal Garrett's posse surrounds the mission at dawn. A fierce shootout erupts through the crumbling cloisters. You corner Tomas, who throws a key at you. "The letter — it's in the bell tower. But McClane's men are already inside! They want to burn it!"

                
You sprint up the stone steps as flames lick the wooden beams. McClane himself is there, holding the coded letter. "This ends now," he snarls, pulling a revolver.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // DEAD HORSE CANYON — THE TRAP SPRUNG //
            

                
The posse takes positions. When the train stops and the gang boards, McClane's men open fire — not on the bandits, but on the posse! "It's a massacre!" the marshal shouts. McClane has double-crossed everyone. You spot Isabella pinned down behind a rock. Buckshot Johnny (if alive) unleashes his shotgun, covering your charge. El Muerto is laughing as he loads the gold onto mules.

                
The canyon fills with gunsmoke and screams. You have one chance to turn the tide.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // MISSION — CANDLELIT CONSPIRACY //
            

                
El Muerto spreads a map on the altar. "The train passes the canyon at dawn. We blow the trestle, board the mail car, and take the gold. McClane will be waiting with his men, but we have a surprise — his own explosives, rigged to a secondary fuse. When he thinks he's got us, we'll blow them to kingdom come."

                
He looks at you. "I want you on the trestle, setting the dynamite. It's the most dangerous job. Do it, and you get a fifth of the gold. Fail, and you're dead. What do you say?"

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // COYOTE CREEK BANK — MIDNIGHT ROBBERY //
            

                
To prove you're not a spy, El Muerto demands you rob the town bank with Tomas. The two of you blow the safe with nitroglycerin. But as you scoop up the cash, Tomas turns on you. "The boss says only one of us walks away." He aims his gun. You have a split second.

                
Outside, a Pinkerton agent's lantern flashes — it's Isabella, signaling. She's found the letter. The bank is about to be surrounded by the marshal's men.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // NIGHTHAWK TRAIL — THE VIGILANTES //
            

                
Buckshot Johnny leads you on a hard ride through the night. Maria, his granddaughter, has slipped word that the gang is gathering at the mission. As you approach, you see the glow of lanterns and hear the strum of a guitar. "They're celebrating," Johnny whispers. "This is our chance." He hands you a stick of dynamite. "For my boy."

                
The two of you sneak close. The mission's powder magazine sits near the old well. One well-placed throw could take out half the gang. But it would also destroy any evidence of McClane's crimes.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // TRESTLE BRIDGE — DAWN EXPLOSION //
            

                
You light the fuse and dive for cover. The trestle explodes in a thunder of fire and splintered wood. The train shrieks, derailing just enough to stop without crashing. The gang swarms the gold car. But McClane's men spring their trap — guns blazing from the canyon rim. El Muerto laughs and triggers the secondary fuse, blowing a rock slide that buries half of McClane's mercenaries.

                
In the chaos, you climb into the gold car. Isabella is there, gun drawn. "Step away from the gold," she orders. El Muerto appears behind her. A three-way standoff.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // MOMENTS BEFORE DAWN — DOUBLE CROSS //
            

                
You slip away from the gang under cover of darkness and race to warn Marshal Garrett. But McClane is waiting. "I knew you'd break," he sneers. "You're just like all the others — no stomach for real work." He raises a shotgun. Before he can fire, Johnny's shotgun booms from the shadows. McClane falls. "Always pay your debts," Johnny growls.

                
Now you have McClane's own ledger, proving the insurance fraud. With Isabella's testimony, you can bring down the railroad empire. But El Muerto is still on his way to the train.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // COYOTE CREEK MAIN STREET — HIGH NOON //
            

                
The townsfolk line the boardwalks. The sun blazes. El Muerto stands at one end of the street, his pearl-handled Colt gleaming. You at the other, hand hovering. "¡Vamanos!" he shouts. "Let's see if you're as fast as they say, gringo."

                
The world goes silent. A wagon creaks. A hawk cries. Your fingers twitch. In a blur, you draw — two shots ring out as one. Dust swirls. One man falls. The other stands.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // ABANDONED SILVER MINE — ENTRANCE COLLAPSING //
            

                
The dynamite goes off, collapsing the mine entrance. Rocks bury the gang's supplies and half their horses. El Muerto, trapped inside, screams curses. You have a choice: dig him out and face him, or leave him to die in the dark. Isabella wants him alive for trial. Johnny wants him dead for his son.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // BELL TOWER — FLAMES AND LEAD //
            

                
You and McClane face each other across the burning bell tower. The letter is on a table between you. "You think you're a hero?" he spits. "Heroes die young." He draws. You're faster. The shot echoes through the valley as McClane tumbles backward into the flames. You snatch the letter and leap from the window, landing in a hay cart as the tower collapses.

                
Below, the town watches in awe. The marshal nods. Isabella smiles. The letter is safe. The conspiracy is exposed.

            

            

                
                
            

        


        
        

            // ENDING: THE HERO OF COYOTE CREEK //
            

                
✦ ENDING: THE SILVER STAR ✦

                
You saved the gold, exposed the conspiracy, and brought El Muerto and McClane to justice. The town erects a statue in your honor. Isabella offers you a job with the Pinkertons. You accept, but only for a while — the West still calls. Every train that passes through Coyote Creek blows its whistle for you.

                
// You chose the law. But the law can be a heavy star to wear. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE NEW BANDIT KING //
            

                
✦ ENDING: EL PATRON ✦

                
With El Muerto gone, the gang looks to you. You become the most wanted man in the territory, robbing trains, banks, and stagecoaches. Your face is on wanted posters from Texas to California. But you also share the wealth with the poor, and the people sing corridos about you. You're an outlaw, but you're a legend.

                
// You traded your soul for gold and glory. It feels lighter than you thought. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE FASTEST GUN //
            

                
✦ ENDING: QUICK AS LIGHTNING ✦

                
You won the duel. Now every gunslinger wants to test themselves against you. You drift from town to town, never staying long, always watching the horizon. Sometimes you stop at a saloon and play a slow game of poker, and someone asks, "Are you the one who outdrew El Muerto?" You just smile and order another whiskey.

                
// The fastest gun in the West has no home. But that suits you fine. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: BURIED IN BOOT HILL //
            

                
✕ ENDING: THE FINAL BULLET ✕

                
You took a bullet, but your sacrifice wasn't in vain. The marshal and Isabella finished the work. The gold was saved, McClane was hanged, and the gang scattered. They buried you on a hill overlooking the canyon, with a stone that reads: "Here lies a stranger who did the right thing." Every spring, desert flowers grow on your grave.

                
// Dying is easy. Making a difference is hard. You did both. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE SHERIFF'S STAR //
            

                
◇ ENDING: PEACE AT LAST ◇

                
Marshal Garrett retires, and the town votes you in as the new sheriff. You clean up Coyote Creek, run the railroad out of town, and marry Isabella. The two of you raise a family on a small ranch outside town. Sometimes, when the train whistle blows, you look up and remember the days of gunfights and gold. But you don't miss them.

                
// Some men chase glory. You found something better: peace. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE GOLDEN GHOST //
            

                
✦ ENDING: ALL THE GOLD IN THE WEST ✦

                
You rode away with the gold. Changed your name, bought a hacienda in Mexico, and lived like a king. But the gold was cursed — every midnight, you hear the screams of the men who died for it. You're rich, but you never sleep through the night. Sometimes you see a skull-faced figure in the mirror.

                
// The gold is yours. And so is the guilt. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE SPIRIT OF THE WEST //
            

                
✦ ENDING: THE MASKED RIDER ✦

                
You vanished after the shootout, taking the coded letter and a mask. Now you roam the territory as a vigilante — striking at corrupt railroads, freeing prisoners, and giving stolen gold to the poor. No one knows your face, but everyone knows your name: The Ghost of Coyote Creek. The law hunts you, but the people protect you. You are the West's last hope.

                
// You chose justice outside the law. It's a lonely road, but it's yours. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: MEXICAN STANDOFF //
            

                
◈ ENDING: NO ONE WINS ◈

                
You, El Muerto, and McClane stand in a triangle, guns drawn. Three shots ring out simultaneously. When the dust clears, all three of you are down. The gold sits untouched in the mail car. The town bury you all side by side on Boot Hill. No one wins — but the gold goes back to the government, and the railroad is investigated. Your death wasn't empty.

                
// Sometimes a draw is the only fair outcome. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: THE PREACHER'S PEACE //
            

                
✙ ENDING: FROM GUNS TO GLORY ✙

                
You lay down your guns and take up the cross. The mission is rebuilt, and you become its caretaker. Townsfolk come to hear your sermons about redemption. You never stop carrying the scar of a bullet near your heart, a reminder that violence can be left behind. Isabella visits often — not as an agent, but as a friend. You've found a different kind of peace.

                
// The fastest gun in the West became its gentlest soul. //

            


        
        

            // ENDING: BLOOD FOR BLOOD //
            

                
✕ ENDING: BURN IT ALL DOWN ✕

                
You left El Muerto to die in the mine and watched the entrance collapse. Johnny wept over his son's grave that night. But the revenge didn't bring peace — it only left a hole. You and Johnny ride together now, haunted by the ghosts of the men you've killed. The West doesn't give clean endings.

                
// Some debts can't be paid with bullets. //
